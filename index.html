Welcome to the Architecture Classification Project

Overview:

If I wasn't a software engineer, I would have been an architect.

This was more of a side project after my training with Daiku. Once I got a hold of the basics of the platform, I decided to explore a topic that was of interest to me personally. As someone who travels in my free time, my favourite thing about the places I visit is the architecture. I always marvel at the structures around me -- from buildings to monuments, everything holds a story and I take a moment to explore every curve and crevice that catches my eye. I think that has a way to mirror the artist who wielded the tool that created what people now take for granted as a simple structure, and just like their architects, all buildings are different. They hold similar styles, sure, albeit they are still very distinct.

My goal in this project was to see what dots a machine would connect that my eye could not between a standard dataset of certain images of various architecture styles. Maybe there are things that are actually similar in classification style between different time periods that a human would not have come to notice right away -- or the contrary -- distinctions that we take for granted as light similarities.
